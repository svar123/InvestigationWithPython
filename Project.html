Project 2: Analysis of Titanic Dataset¶

The sinking of the RMS Titanic is one of the most infamous shipwrecks in history. On April 15, 1912, during her maiden voyage, the Titanic sank after colliding with an iceberg, killing 1502 out of 2224 passengers and crew.

This dataset contains demographics and passenger information from 891 of the 2224 passengers and crew on board the Titanic.

This report will analyze the data set and try to answer the following questions: What factors made the people more likely to survive? Did being male or female help to survive? Was it being in 1st class? Did age play a factor?

The above violin plot shows that more females survived than males. Let's use a statistical test to verify this. The test I will use is Chi-square for independence since the data is categorical and based on frequency/count.

The null hypothesis is that survival is independent of gender. The alternate hypothesis is that there is an association between gender and survival. Conditions for Chi-square are met:

  1. The data is frequency/count
  2. The expected counts are greater than 5
  3. The dataset is assumed to be population.

Conclusions:

These conclusions are based on the given dataset (891 out of 2224 total passengers) and could be skewed.

  1. Out of 891 passengers, 342 passengers survived and 549 died.
  2. Passengers between 25 and 35 years of age had higher survival rates.
  3. Survival was higher for females and for passengers having no siblings or spouse or parents or children.
  4. Survival was higher for passengers in first class.
  5. Regarding fares paid and port of embarkation, we cannot really say that survival was probably higher because most of the passengers paid low fare and originated from Southampton.

Statistical tests were done to verify that survival was greater for females and also for first class passengers.
